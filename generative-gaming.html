Generative Gaming

The traditional model of gaming—high labor costs, rigid storylines, and static assets—is obsolete. We are investing in the shift toward "Dream Engines": platforms where content is not rendered, but imagined in real-time.

The Investment Perspective: We believe the greatest value capture in the next decade will be in tools that allow games to build themselves.

Focus Areas:

Autonomous Agents: NPCs powered by LLMs that possess long-term memory, distinct psychology, and agency, creating non-deterministic gameplay.

Procedural Reality: Infrastructure that reduces 3D asset creation from days to milliseconds via text-to-mesh and AI-driven physics.

Personalized Engines: Games that adapt their difficulty, narrative, and environment in real-time based on the player's biometric and behavioral data.

02. Cognitive Infrastructure

The Rails of the Synthetic Economy.

Generative worlds require exponential compute and novel architecture. We invest in the "picks and shovels" that allow the generative future to run at scale without latency.

The Investment Perspective: As inference costs drop, the demand for specialized, verifiable compute will skyrocket.

Focus Areas:

Decentralized Compute Networks: Distributed GPU grids that power high-fidelity generative rendering.

Verifiable Inference: Cryptographic proofs ensuring AI outcomes are fair and untampered with.

Edge AI Layers: Models optimized to run locally on devices, reducing cloud dependency for real-time interactions.

03. Spatial & Neural Interfaces

Dissolving the Screen.

As digital worlds become infinitely complex, the hardware used to perceive them must evolve. We back the hardware and software layers that bridge the biological eye with the digital signal.

The Investment Perspective: The screen is a temporary barrier. The future interface is immersive, ambient, and direct.

Focus Areas:

Next-Gen Haptics: Feedback systems that simulate weight, texture, and temperature in virtual environments.

Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI): Non-invasive neural controllers for hands-free navigation.

Light-Field Displays: Visual technology that eliminates the friction and nausea of current VR/AR optics.
